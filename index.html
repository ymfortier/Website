Following the footsteps of my ancestors in Secwépemc’ulucw - the land of the Secwepemc

My name is Yvonne Fortier, a member of the Tk'emlups te Secwépemc, with my roots entrenched in Chu Chua, where I spent my childhood. I am Secwepemc (Shuswap) and my people live within a vast territory in the south central part of the province of British Columbia; approximately 180,000 square km. The landscape is everchanging - mountains, lakes, creeks, forests, desert lands,and the natural wonders of Mother Nature.

I have travelled to places such as Singapore, Sydney, Manchester, London, Dublin, Los Angelos, Las Vegas, and loved sightseeing and experiencing different cultures. Now I want to explore my homelands - Secwépemc’ulucw, and places my parents and Elders shared in their stories. I acknowledge the people whose footsteps I may follow, and respect all the living beings around me.

I welcome you to share my journey. I share with you an audio clip by the late Nels Mitchell, singing for you The Welcome Song in Secwépemctsin.
