Following the footsteps of my ancestors in Secwépemc’ulucw - the land of the Secwepemc

My name is Yvonne Fortier, a member of the Tk'emlups te Secwépemc, with my roots entrenched in Chu Chua, where I spent my childhood. I am Secwepemc (Shuswap) and my people live within a vast territory in the south central part of the province of British Columbia; approximately 180,000 square km. The landscape is everchanging - mountains, lakes, rivers, creeks, forests, desert lands,and the natural wonders of Mother Nature.

I have travelled to places such as Singapore, Sydney, Manchester, London, Dublin, Los Angelos, Las Vegas, and loved sightseeing and experiencing different cultures. Now I want to explore my homelands - Secwépemc’ulucw, and places my parents and Elders shared in their stories. I acknowledge the people whose footsteps I may follow, and respect all the living beings around me.

Welcome to the land of the Secwepemc; usually drummers and singers welcome you to their land with the Welcome Song. The following audio clip is by the late Nels Mitchell, singing The Welcome Song in Secwépemctsin.

1. North Thompson Valley - Heli Tour

One of my dreams is to book a helicopter tour of the North Thompson Valley, as I understand it is a breath-taking scenic tour and an experience like no other. I have a healthy respect, and at times fear, for extreme heights such as Mount Robson offers; however, I am sure I will overcome that fear and enjoy my tour.
